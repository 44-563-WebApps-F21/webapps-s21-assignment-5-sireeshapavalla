    Ant
    
              
    Ants are eusocial insects of the family Formicidae and, along with the related wasps and bees, belong to the order Hymenoptera. Ants appear in the fossil record across the globe in considerable diversity during the latest early Late Cretaceous,  suggesting an earlier origin.

    Bat
    
              
    Bats are mammals of the order Chiroptera.[a] With their forelimbs adapted as wings, they are the only mammals capable of true and sustained flight. Bats are more manoeuvrable than most birds, flying with their very long spread-out digits covered with a thin membrane or patagium

    Lion
    
              
    The lion (Panthera leo) is a large felid of the genus Panthera native to Africa and India. It has a muscular, deep-chested body, short, rounded head, round ears, and a hairy tuft at the end of its tail. It is sexually dimorp . It is a social species, forming groups called prides
